Sizes

Everything scales based on the body fluid font size. Using the classes .tiny or .small on the body tag everything will be smaller then normal.

Container

The container makes sure there is some margin between the page and its content. It never grows larger then 1280 pixels. It is used by wrapping content in a .container element.

Margins and paddings

Margins and paddings are default 8px but are also available in 16, 24 and 40 pixels. For margin (or paddings) in a all directions the classes .margins, .margins-16, .margins-24 and .margins-40 are available. (Same applies to paddings)

For margins and paddings on the horizontal or vertical side the classes .margins-vertical and .margins-horizontal can be used. These can also be extended with -16, -24 or -40 for making the margins larger. (Same applies to paddings)

For margins and paddings in a specific direction .margin-top, .margin-right, .margin-bottom and .margin-left can be used. These also work with the -16, -24 or -40 extensions. (Same applies to paddings)

Visibility

Sometimes it is needed that elements hide or become visible on different screen sizes. The classes .hidden, .visible and .visible-inline can be used for it.

These classes work with the breakpoints mobile, tablet, laptop and pc and the appending of up and down. So .hidden-tablet will hide the element on tablets only. .hidden-tablet-up will hide the element on tablet and larger screens. Logically .hidden-tablet-down will hide the element on tablet and smaller screens.

The difference between .visible and .visible-inline is that .visible uses the css 'display: block;' and .visible-inline the css 'display: inline-block;'.
